Stave 1: Marley's Ghost

Key Characters Here

  • Scrooge
  • Bob Cratchit
  • Fred

Scrooge's Emotional State

At the beginning, Scrooge is cold, tight-fisted, and solitary as an oyster. He aggressively rejects Christmas cheer ("Humbug!"). When faced with Marley's ghost, his wall of disbelief begins to crack, introducing fear for the first time.

Stave 3: The Ghost of Christmas Present

Key Characters Here

  • Ghost of Present
  • Tiny Tim
  • The Cratchit Family
  • Fred's Party Guests

Scrooge's Emotional State

Scrooge sees the joy of Christmas in others, despite their poverty. He feels genuine concern when he learns Tiny Tim might die. He also sees people mocking him at his nephew's party. He is learning empathy and feeling shame.

Stave 4: The Ghost of Christmas Yet to Come

Key Characters Here

  • The Silent Phantom
  • Businessmen
  • Thieves
  • Dead Scrooge

Scrooge's Emotional State

This is the climax of his fear. He sees that nobody mourns his death; they only care about his money. Seeing his own neglected gravestone terrifies him into a desperate desire to change his fate. He is fully humbled.

Stave 5: The End of It

Key Characters Here

  • Redeemed Scrooge
  • The Turkey Boy
  • Everyone he meets

Scrooge's Emotional State

Scrooge wakes up reborn. He is giddy with happiness, generous (buying the prize turkey), and reconnects with his family. He becomes a "second father" to Tiny Tim and keeps Christmas in his heart all year round.
