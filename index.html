Profile: Ebenezer Scrooge

Select a Chapter to see his emotional change:

Stave 1 (The Miser) Stave 2 (Regret) Stave 3 (Empathy) Stave 4 (Fear) Stave 5 (Redemption)

Stave 1: Marley's Ghost

Emotion: Cold, Mean, Stubborn

Scrooge is at his worst. He hates Christmas ("Humbug!"), refuses to help the poor, and treats his clerk Bob badly. Even when he sees Marley's ghost, he tries not to believe it.

Stave 2: The Past

Emotion: Sadness, Nostalgia, Regret

Scrooge sees himself as a lonely boy. He cries for the first time. He sees Belle breaking up with him because of his greed. He starts to wish he had been nicer.

Stave 3: The Present

Emotion: Guilt, Care, Shame

Scrooge sees Tiny Tim and learns he might die. He asks the Spirit "Will he live?". He feels ashamed when he hears his own mean words used against him.

Stave 4: The Future

Emotion: Terror, Desperation

Scrooge sees his own neglected grave. He realizes that money cannot buy love. He falls on his knees and promises to change his life if he gets one more chance.

Stave 5: The End

Emotion: Joy, Generosity, Love

Scrooge wakes up on Christmas morning. He is giddy as a schoolboy! He buys the turkey for Bob, laughs with Fred, and becomes a second father to Tiny Tim.

Profile: The Three Spirits

  • Ghost of Christmas Past: Looks like a child and an old man. Shows memories.
  • Ghost of Christmas Present: A giant, jolly man in a green robe. Shows joy and poverty.
  • Ghost of Christmas Yet to Come: A dark phantom who does not speak. Shows death.

Profile: The Cratchit Family

  • Bob Cratchit: Scrooge's clerk. Poor but happy.
  • Tiny Tim: Bob's crippled son. He says "God bless us, every one!"
  • Mrs. Cratchit: Bob's wife. She doesn't like Scrooge but toasts him anyway.
